The TACT Method

In the modern era, machine learning and data science is quickly becoming an essential piece of a service or product. The TACT method is a way to:

  • Meet *customer need* without guessing
  • Deliver high quality models that have less defects
  • Reduce *cost* of hiring machine learning experts that work within vacuums
  • Increase *communication* between product stakeholders
  • Show the results and get buy in

Background

The contributors to this have been directly involved with delivering hundreds of machine learning models, data science visualizations and data pipelines. We have seen the high interest credit card debt that google talked about with machine learning projects first hand.

After years of struggling to remember models like CRISP-DM (insert others) we came up with TACT which simply stands for Target, Arrange, Compose, and Transmit. This balances both the production with the evaluation aspects of all machine learning and data science projects.

The goal of course is to reduce rework, work in progress, waiting, skills, time, communication overhead, over production, and delivering results less than what the customer wants.

1. Target

Machine learning and data science can have the the unfortunate side effect of being a solution looking for problems. The first step of the TACT method is to understand the customer need well.

2. Arrange

Data science without data is just science. That means the second step is to arrange data pieces but only the essential data. This means sorting through data and selecting only what's relevant, straightening the data whether that's melting or unmelting the data frames, and finally cleaning up data for usage.

3. Compose

Data science is a focus on modeling and coming up with insight from the arranged data and information. This means putting our data either through statistics, traditional machine learning models, or fancy deep learning architectures. The focus is finding the simplest model to make the standard.

4. Transmit

Finally comes the point where the insight that is arrived upon is transmitted to either internal or external customers. This means deploying the code to a staging or production server as well as presenting the results to the stakeholders in the project.
